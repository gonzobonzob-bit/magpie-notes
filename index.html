⚡ 120% Rule — MPU Avoidance
Enclosure/Bus ×1.2 − Breaker =
Anything above 0.0A needs one of these instead of a straight 120% pass:
  • Supply-side (line-side) tap — connects PV ahead of the main breaker, so the 120% busbar rule doesn't apply at all.
  • Load-side tap — adds the PV breaker after the main breaker; needs available busbar space rated for it.
  • Sum of all breakers rule — NEC 705.12(B)(2)(3)(b) alternative: total of every breaker (existing + PV) can't exceed the busbar rating, no MSP upgrade required.
  • MPU (Main Panel Upgrade) — replace/upsize the panel and main breaker so the busbar rating supports the PV breaker outright; always works, but it's the costliest and slowest path.
Reminder: this is the MPU avoidance section — the first three options let you skip the upgrade when one applies; MPU is just the fallback when none of them do.
